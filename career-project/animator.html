Animator

by Mia

Job Description

A traditional animator draws a series of images. Images are then taken to create the illusion of movement. People in this career are part storytellers, part illustrators, and part filmmakers. An average project has numerous stages. To start, animators come up with new concepts. Either they or a team could carry out this. They create storyboards next. These represent their ideas in pictorial form. They produce the visuals that will appear in the finished product when an idea has been chosen.

Animators may draw by hand and scan the drawings into the computer for this process. Or they may use a computer to make the full animation. They can create intricate 3D models in this way.

These professionals then animate their pictures. Their projects range from being quite simple to being extremely sophisticated. Sometimes all they need to do is make a logo scroll over the screen. To animate 3D objects like dinosaurs and spacecraft, they could collaborate with a team.

Education and Training

If you want to get into digital animation, there are many paths you can take. The key to getting work in the field is to have a great demo reel. A post-secondary program in art or design will give you the skills to make one.

These programs last from 1 to 4 years. Depending on the program, you will earn a certificate, an associate degree, or a bachelor's degree. Many animators have a bachelor's degree.

It's a good idea to choose a program that offers an internship. This will give you work experience in your area of focus. You'll also get many chances to develop your demo reel. Some major animation studios offer their own in-house training programs.

Earnings

Incomes in this field vary widely. In general, animators earn between $41,000 and $124,000 a year. The median income is about $73,000 a year. Earnings depend on their reputation and experience. Their location and the amount they work also impact income. Info from xello.

Core Tasks

  • Brainstorm new ideas
  • Create and animate graphics using computers
  • Produce storyboards, which visually outline how ideas can be developed
  • Projects range from animating a commercial logo to creating a movie's 3D world
  • Some create 3D models of structures for architectural firms

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for film companies, filmmakers, TV stations, video game developers, or ad companies
  • Work indoors in offices and production studios
  • Long hours at a computer or design board may cause back, neck, wrist, or eye strain
  • Often work alone, and may talk to other animators, producers, or filmmakers
  • Pressure to be creative can be stressful

Hiring Opportinities

Local companies that may hire people in this career include, SM&A, CrowdPharm, Intrepid Studios, and Randstad.

Sample Career Paths

Entry-Level Computer Animator-$40,000 - $55,000

Requirments

  1. Degree in animation or a related subject
  2. Demo reel
  3. Create animation under the supervision of a more senior animator
  4. Perform administrative duties
Computer Animator-$50,000 - $75,000

Requirments

  1. 2 years of experience in computer animation
  2. Do difficult animation projects
  3. Come up with creative ideas
  4. Make storyboards to go with those ideas
Senior Animator-$65,000 - $115,000

Requirments

  1. Possibly some further training, such as a business degree
  2. 3 to 5 years of experience in computer animation
  3. Generate creative ideas
  4. Meet with clients
  5. Calculate budgets
  6. Manage projects and schedules
  7. Do some animation
President/Vice President Of An Animation Studio-$100,000 - $200,000

Requirments

  1. Network of clients
  2. 5 to 10 years of experience in computer animation
  3. Further training, such as a business degree
  4. Hire and fire personnel
  5. Meet with clients
  6. Direct and manage the company
  7. Manage projects and schedules

Future Growth and Demands

Employment of special effects artists and animators is projected to grow 5 percent from 2021 to 2031, about as fast as the average for all occupations.
